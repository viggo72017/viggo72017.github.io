WARM, REALISTIC, STORY
by Charles Polinger (reviewed in the United States on April 21, 2018)

"One of my favorite movies, about a young boy and his relationship with a film operator. This movie harks back to the days when movie houses had projectionists, and it is easy to imagine how they learned techniques to make their jobs more efficient. It is wonderful to see the young boy's admiration for the projectionist, and desire to learn "the trade." Of course, the young boy does not stay young forever, and Cinema Paradiso follows the aging of both the projectionist and the boy. I liked the simple story, the unique subject, the small-town atmosphere and the acting. It was an easy film to follow, unlike many today that have xx number of characters and intertwining stories."
